Preprocessing itself takes place in several logically successive phases that may, in a particular implementation, be condensed.



1. 1st, trigraph sequences are replaced by their equivalents. Shoul the operating system environment require it, 'newline' characters are introduced between the lines of the source file.
2. Each occurance of a backslash character '\' followed by a 'newline' is deleted thus aplicing lines.
3. The program is split into tokens seperated by white-space characters. Comments are replaced by a single space. Then preprocessing directives are obeyed and macros are expanded.
4. Escape sequences in character constants and string literals are replaced by their equivalents. Then adjacent string literals ar concatenated.
5. The result is translated then linked together with other programs and libraries by collecting the necessary programs and data and connecting external function and object references to their definitions.
