The grammer has undefined terminal ymbols integer-constant, character-constant, floating-constant, identifier, string and enumeration-constant. The typewriter style words and symbols are terminals given literally.
This grammer can be transformed mechanically into input acceptable to an automatic parser-generator. Besides adding whatever syntatic marking is used to indicate alternatives in productions, it is necessary to expand the "one of" construction and to duplicate each production with an opt symbol, once with the symbol and once without.
With one further change, namely deleting the production 'typedef-name:identifier' and making 'typedef-name' a terminal symbol, this grammer is acceptable to the 'YACC' parser-generator. It has only one conflict generated by the 'if-else' ambiguity.
