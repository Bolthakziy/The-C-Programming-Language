float y[4][3] = {
  {1, 3, 5},
  {2, 4, 6},
  {3, 5, 7},
};



'y' is a completely-bracketed initialization. 1, 3 and 5 initialize the first row of th array y[0], namely y[0][0], y[0][1] and y[0][2]. Likewise the next 2 lines initialize y[1] and y[2]. The initializer ends early and therefore the elements of y[3] are initialized with '0'. Precisely the same effect could have been achieved by

float y[4][3] = {1, 3, 5, 2, 4, 6, 3, 5, 7};

The initilaizer for y begins with a left brace but that y[0] does not. Therefore three elements from the list are used. Likewise the next three are taken successively for y[1] and then for y[2]. Also

float y[4][3] = {{1}, {2}, {3}, {4}};

intializes the first column of y and leaves the rest 0.
