In the 'for' statement, the first expression is evaluated before each iteration and if it becomes equal to '0', the 'for' is terminated. The 3rd expression is evaluated after each iteration and thus specifies a re-initialization for the loop.

There is no restriction on its type. Side-effects from each expression are completed immediately after its evaluation.

If the substatement does not contain 'continue', a statement



for (expression1; expression2; expression3) statement



is equivalent to



expression1;
while (expression2) {
  statement
  expression3;
}



Any of the three expressions may be dropped. A missing 2nd expression makes the implied test equivalent to testing a non-zero constant.
